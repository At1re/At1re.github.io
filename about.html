Our Brotherhood

The Gamma Epsilon Chapter of Pi Kappa Alpha at Utah State University is built on the four pillars that have defined PIKE since 1868: Scholarship, Leadership, Athletics, and Gentlemanly Conduct. We hold ourselves to the highest standard — on campus, in the community, and in life.

Our brothers are scholars, athletes, leaders, and lifelong friends. From Aggie athletics to the Interfraternity Council to Cache Valley service projects, Gamma Epsilon is active in every corner of Utah State campus life.

We carry the legacy of Pi Kappa Alpha — founded on March 1, 1868 at the University of Virginia — and honor that tradition every day in Logan, Utah. As members of one of North America's largest and most respected fraternities, Gamma Epsilon brothers join a network of over 313,000 lifetime initiates who have gone on to lead in business, government, athletics, and public service.

Brotherhood at Gamma Epsilon isn't a marketing phrase. It's a commitment we make to each other — to push each other to be better, hold each other accountable, and show up when it matters. When you're a Pike, you always have a place to belong.
