Why Rush PIKE?

Pi Kappa Alpha is one of the largest and most prestigious fraternities in North America — and Gamma Epsilon at Utah State is one of its strongest chapters. As a brother, you'll join a group of men who take their commitments seriously: in class, in life, and to each other.

Our chapter has a proven track record of academic excellence, campus leadership, athletic achievement, and community service. Brothers leave USU with lifelong friendships, professional networks, and a foundation for success that doesn't stop at graduation.

We're intentional about who we bring in, because we believe in quality. We're looking for men who lead, who follow through, and who want to be part of something bigger than themselves. If that sounds like you — come find out what Gamma Epsilon is about.

The Rush Process

1
Attend a Rush Event
Come to one of our open events and meet the brothers. No pressure, no commitment — just good people and a real look at what PIKE is about.
2
Get to Know the Brotherhood
Attend multiple events throughout Rush. Brothers get to know you; you get to know them. Rush is mutual — we're both figuring out if it's the right fit.
3
Receive a Bid
If it's a mutual match, you'll receive an official invitation to join the new member class of Gamma Epsilon. This is the beginning, not the end.
4
New Member Period
Learn the history, values, and traditions of Pi Kappa Alpha alongside your pledge brothers. This is where the real brotherhood starts to form.
5
Initiation
Welcome to the brotherhood. Once initiated, you're a brother of Pi Kappa Alpha for life. Gamma Epsilon will always be part of you.

Common Questions

Do I need to be a freshman to rush?
No. We welcome men at any point in their academic career. Transfer students and upperclassmen are absolutely welcome — some of our best brothers rushed as juniors.
What GPA do I need?
We have a minimum GPA requirement for membership. Reach out directly for current specifics — but we're looking for men who take academics seriously.
What does membership cost?
Chapter dues cover operations, events, philanthropy, and national fees. Payment plans are available. Finances should never be a barrier for a strong candidate.
How much time does it take?
Brotherhood takes time — but it's designed to enhance your college experience. Chapter meetings, service, and events are expected. You control the rest.
What makes PIKE different?
Our four pillars — Scholarship, Leadership, Athletics, and Gentlemanly Conduct — aren't marketing. They're standards we actually hold ourselves to. Come see it for yourself.
