Rush Pi Kappa Alpha

Begin your journey as a brother of Gamma Epsilon

Why Rush PIKE?

Pi Kappa Alpha is one of the largest and most prestigious fraternities in North America — and Gamma Epsilon at Utah State is one of its strongest chapters. As a brother, you'll join a brotherhood of men who take their commitments seriously: in class, in life, and to each other.

Our chapter has a proven track record of academic excellence, campus leadership, athletic achievement, and community service. Brothers leave USU with lifelong friendships, professional networks, and a foundation for success that doesn't stop when you graduate.

We're selective, because we believe in quality. We're looking for men who lead, who follow through, and who want to be part of something bigger than themselves. If that sounds like you — come find out what Gamma Epsilon is about.

The Rush Process

1
Attend a Rush Event
Show up to one of our open rush events and meet the brothers of Gamma Epsilon. No pressure, no commitment — just good people and a chance to see what we're about.
2
Get to Know the Brotherhood
Attend multiple events throughout Rush Week. Brothers will get to know you, and you'll get to know them. Rush is mutual — we're both figuring out if it's the right fit.
3
Receive a Bid
If it's a mutual match, you'll receive a bid — an official invitation to join the new member class of Gamma Epsilon. This is the beginning, not the end.
4
New Member Period
Learn the history, values, and traditions of Pi Kappa Alpha alongside your pledge brothers. This is where the real brotherhood starts to form.
5
Initiation
Welcome to the brotherhood. Once you're initiated, you're a brother of Pi Kappa Alpha for life. Gamma Epsilon will be part of you — and you'll always have a place in it.

Common Questions

Do I need to be a freshman to rush?
No. We accept men at any point in their academic career. Transfer students and upperclassmen are absolutely welcome — some of our best brothers rushed as juniors.
What GPA do I need?
We take scholarship seriously and have a GPA requirement for membership. Reach out for current specifics — but we're looking for men who take academics seriously, regardless of their exact number.
What does it cost to be a member?
Chapter dues cover operations, events, philanthropy, and national fees. Payment plans are available. We never want finances to be a barrier for a good man who wants to join.
How much of a time commitment is it?
Brotherhood takes time — but it's designed to enhance your college experience, not detract from it. Chapter meetings, events, and service hours are expectations. You set your own pace beyond that.
What makes PIKE different from other fraternities?
Our four pillars — Scholarship, Leadership, Athletics, and Gentlemanly Conduct — aren't just marketing. They're standards we actually hold ourselves to. Come meet the brothers and see it for yourself.
