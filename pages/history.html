The Story of Gamma Epsilon

March 1, 1868

National Founding at U.Va.

Pi Kappa Alpha was founded in Room 47 of West Range at the University of Virginia by six men who believed a fraternity should stand for something real. Frederick Southall, Julian Wood, James Deane, Littleton Redd, William Shields, and Robertson Howard built something that would outlast them by centuries. Their guiding principle: Friendship, Love, and Truth.
Late 1800s – Early 1900s

Southern Roots, National Reach

Pi Kappa Alpha began its first century as a distinctly Southern fraternity, with deep roots in the culture and traditions of the American South. As the 20th century opened, the fraternity began an intentional national expansion — planting chapters from coast to coast and building a network of brothers who carried the garnet and old gold everywhere they went.
Mid 20th Century

Growth & Consolidation

PIKE grew steadily through the mid-century, becoming one of the largest and most respected fraternities in North America. The national organization developed rigorous standards for chapter operations, scholarship, and leadership that set Pi Kappa Alpha apart from other fraternities.
Est.

Gamma Epsilon Chartered at USU

The Gamma Epsilon Chapter was chartered at Utah State University in Logan, Utah — bringing the traditions and values of Pi Kappa Alpha to the Aggies of Cache Valley. From the beginning, the chapter set out to be among the strongest Greek organizations on campus.
Decades of Brotherhood

Building the Gamma Epsilon Legacy

Generation after generation, Gamma Epsilon brothers have made their mark at Utah State. Chapter alumni have gone on to careers in business, law, medicine, engineering, government, and the military. Each pledge class inherits a legacy and adds to it — leaving the chapter better than they found it.
Today

Gamma Epsilon Now

Today, the brothers of Gamma Epsilon carry the garnet and old gold with the same pride as those first six founders. We honor what came before us, and we build on it — competing in intramurals, leading in IFC, excelling in the classroom, serving Cache Valley, and welcoming new brothers into a brotherhood that will last the rest of their lives.
