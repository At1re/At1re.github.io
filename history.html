The Story of Gamma Epsilon

March 1, 1868
National Founding at the University of Virginia
Pi Kappa Alpha was founded in Room 47 of West Range at the University of Virginia by six young men who believed a fraternity should stand for something real. Frederick Southall, Julian Wood, James Deane, Littleton Redd, William Shields, and Robertson Howard built something that would outlast them by centuries. Their guiding principle: Friendship, Love, and Truth.
Late 1800s – Early 1900s
Southern Roots, National Ambition
Pi Kappa Alpha began its first decades as a distinctly Southern fraternity, with deep roots in the culture and traditions of the American South. As the 20th century dawned, the fraternity began a deliberate national expansion — planting chapters from coast to coast and building a network of brothers who carried the garnet and old gold everywhere they went.
Mid 20th Century
Growth, Standards & National Distinction
PIKE grew steadily into one of the largest and most respected fraternities in North America. The national organization developed rigorous standards for chapter operations, scholarship, and leadership — standards that set Pi Kappa Alpha apart and remain core to who we are today.
Est.
Gamma Epsilon Chartered at Utah State
The Gamma Epsilon Chapter was chartered at Utah State University in Logan, Utah — bringing the traditions and values of Pi Kappa Alpha to the Aggies of Cache Valley. From the beginning, the chapter set out to be among the strongest Greek organizations on campus, and it has lived up to that standard ever since.
Decades of Brotherhood
Building the Gamma Epsilon Legacy
Generation after generation, Gamma Epsilon brothers have made their mark at Utah State. Chapter alumni have gone on to careers in business, law, medicine, engineering, government, and the military. Each pledge class inherits the legacy that came before them — and adds to it, leaving the chapter stronger than they found it.
Today
Gamma Epsilon Now
Today, the brothers of Gamma Epsilon carry the garnet and old gold with the same pride as those six founders in 1868. We honor what came before us, and we build on it — competing in intramurals, leading in IFC, excelling in the classroom, serving Cache Valley, and welcoming new brothers into a brotherhood that lasts a lifetime.
